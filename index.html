Course Purpose

This course supports Iyawos as they prepare to come off the mat and enter the next stage of spiritual responsibility. The mat has served as womb, shrine, school, and sacred covering. Coming off the mat is not the end of initiation; it is the beginning of disciplined priestly living.

The course emphasizes humility, Iwa, elder relationship, boundaries, service, continued study, divination, and the careful transition from protected spiritual formation into public life.

The mat prepared the priest. The world will test the priest. Iwa must crown the priest.

Module 1 • Week 1

Remembering the Mat: What Was Planted?

The first week invites the Iyawo to look back with reverence. The mat was not only a place of rest or restriction. It was a sacred container where old patterns were interrupted, new spiritual identity was planted, and the relationship with Orisha was deepened.

Teaching Goals

  • Understand the mat as womb, shrine, school, and spiritual container.
  • Reflect on what initiation planted in the Ori and spirit.
  • Identify what must be protected after coming off the mat.
  • Distinguish initiation from transformation.

Key Topics

  • The mat as rebirth.
  • Why restrictions mattered.
  • What changed in body, mind, spirit, and destiny.
  • Humility after spiritual elevation.
  • Protecting what Orisha planted.

Reflection Questions

  • What did the mat teach me?
  • What part of me had to die?
  • What part of me was reborn?
  • What am I still resisting?
  • What has my Orisha been shaping in me?

Divination Integration

Use Obi, four cowries, or the divination method permitted by the elder.

Question:

What lesson from my Iyawo year must I carry forward most carefully?

Practice Assignment: Write a one-page reflection titled:

“What the Mat Gave Me, What the Mat Took From Me, and What I Must Now Protect.”
Module 2 • Week 2

Character After Initiation: Iwa as the Real Crown

The second week teaches that the proof of initiation is not title, clothing, beads, public recognition, or spiritual vocabulary. The true crown is Iwa. The Iyawo must learn to carry Ashe with restraint, humility, patience, and clean speech.

Teaching Goals

  • Understand Iwa as the visible fruit of initiation.
  • Recognize spiritual arrogance and avoid it.
  • Practice speech discipline and emotional restraint.
  • Prepare for conflict without losing character.

SGI Pillar Connection

Words of Power

The initiated person must learn that speech carries Ashe. A priest must speak carefully, truthfully, and with restraint.

Key Topics

  • Iwa Pele as post-initiation responsibility.
  • The danger of “I know now” energy.
  • How to receive correction.
  • Managing conflict with elders, god-siblings, and family.
  • Speech as spiritual technology.

Reflection Questions

  • Has initiation made me softer or harder?
  • Do I listen better than before?
  • Where does pride still hide in me?
  • What old behavior could damage my new life?
  • What does my Orisha require from my character?
Practice Assignment: Create a personal Post-Mat Code of Conduct with 7 vows.

Example: “I will not use initiation as superiority.”
Example: “I will continue learning before teaching.”
Divination Question: What character trait must I strengthen as I come off the mat?
Module 3 • Week 3

Re-entering the World: Boundaries, Service, and Visibility

The third week helps the Iyawo return to family, work, community, godfamily, and public life without losing spiritual alignment. Coming off the mat requires discernment. Not everything sacred should become visible, posted, explained, or defended.

Teaching Goals

  • Develop a healthy re-entry plan.
  • Understand what should remain private.
  • Build strong boundaries around Ori and Orisha.
  • Serve without overexposure or performance.

SGI Pillar Connection

Radical Devotion

Devotion is not performance. Devotion is consistency, reverence, and alignment when no one is watching.

Re-entry Planning Table

Area Boundary Needed Sacred Action
Family What I will and will not explain. Speak calmly, simply, and without argument.
Work How I maintain spiritual discipline in ordinary life. Practice punctuality, ethics, prayer, and patience.
Social Media What I will and will not post. Avoid sacred overexposure and spiritual performance.
Community How I serve without ego. Show up humbly and consistently.
Self-Care How I protect Ori. Rest, cleanse, pray, reflect, and consult divination.

Reflection Questions

  • What parts of my spiritual life are public?
  • What parts must remain private?
  • Who supports my destiny?
  • Who drains my Ori?
  • How do I serve without losing myself?

Divination Integration

Question:

Where do I need stronger boundaries as I re-enter the world?

Practice Assignment: Create a written Re-entry Plan for family, work, social media, community, and personal spiritual care.
Module 4 • Week 4

Walking Forward: Priesthood, Accountability, and Legacy

The final week frames post-mat life as a lifelong path of accountability, study, service, and legacy. Coming off the mat does not mean coming out of discipline. The Iyawo becomes a continuing student of Orisha, elder wisdom, Odu, ritual ethics, song, prayer, and service.

Teaching Goals

  • Understand priesthood as service, not status.
  • Create a 12-month post-mat spiritual plan.
  • Identify accountability structures.
  • Commit to ongoing study and elder relationship.

SGI Pillar Connection

Infinite Wisdom

The Iyawo becomes a lifelong student. Wisdom grows through practice, humility, correction, reflection, and service.

12-Month Post-Mat Plan

Category Commitment
Daily Practice Prayer, Ori care, gratitude, reflection, and disciplined conduct.
Weekly Practice Divination, study, elder check-in, and service.
Monthly Practice Cleansing, offering, community contribution, and review.
Study Focus Orisha, Odu, songs, prayers, ritual ethics, and lineage teachings.
Accountability Elder, mentor, god-sibling, spiritual friend, or study circle.
Service How I will serve without ego or spiritual performance.
Protection How I will guard my Ori, boundaries, speech, and energy.

Reflection Questions

  • What kind of priest am I becoming?
  • Who holds me accountable?
  • What must I study next?
  • How will I serve my Orisha?
  • What legacy do I want to leave?

Final Divination Integration

Question:

What is my next right step after coming off the mat?

Final Assignment: Complete your 12-month post-mat spiritual plan and prepare a 3–5 minute statement beginning with:

“As I come off the mat, I commit to…”

May your Orisha guide your steps.
May your Iwa become your crown.
May your speech carry Ashe.
May your service honor the ancestors.
May you rise from the mat and walk with wisdom.
